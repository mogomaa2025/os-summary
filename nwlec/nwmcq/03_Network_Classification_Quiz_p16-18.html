How is "Network Topology" defined on the slides?
Explanation
Page 17 defines Network Topology as "how devices are connected (shape) and how message flow from one device to another device." Page 16 also states it is "How the computer are connected".
Source: Pages 16 & 17
3
A network confined to a "small geographical area" like a school, campus, or office building is known as a...
Explanation
A LAN is defined as "a group of computers connected in small geographical area such as school, university campus or office building".
Source: Page 18
4
What is a defining characteristic of a Wide Area Network (WAN)?
Explanation
The document states, "WAN can contain multiple smaller networks, such as LANs or MANs." It also notes WANs have "Very low Speed".
Source: Page 18
5
Which of the following is given as the primary example of a WAN?
Explanation
The last point under the WAN description explicitly states: "example of WAN is Internet".
Source: Page 18
6
What type of administrative control is associated with a WAN?
Explanation
Page 18 specifies that a WAN is "Under your ISP Administrative control", while a LAN is under local "administrative Control".
Source: Page 18
7
A network with "High speed of communications" is a characteristic of a...
Explanation
"High speed of communications" is listed as a feature of LANs. In contrast, WANs are described as having "Very low Speed".
Source: Page 18
8
True or False: Network Topology describes the size of the network, such as how many kilometers it covers. TRUE/FALSE
Explanation
False. Network Topology is about "How the computer are connected" (the shape/layout). The size of the network relates to the "Covered Area" classification (e.g., LAN/WAN).
